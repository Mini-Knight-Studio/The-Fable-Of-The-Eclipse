The Solari and Lunari are two factions of the same society. They all live near the Folk Tree City. Due to their natural characteristics as different kinds of animals, they have different roles. The Solari tend to do activities that are related to daytime - gather food, build, craft, and tend the city. The Lunari, in contrast, tend to do activities that are related with nighttime - rituals, hunting, and defending the town. The Solari wake up with the light of the sun, and go to sleep whenever the moon shines. The Lunari wake up as the moon lights up, and go to sleep whenever the sun appears in the sky.

While different in nature, they used to be in harmony and lived together peacefully. But as time went on, they both diverged and became increasingly different. The Solari started becoming more refined and stopped living on burrows, tree branches, and so on, while the Lunari mostly remained the same and came to understand nature better.

This separation of ways of being, changed this society from a homogeneous one into a more heterogeneous one. And this is because of the following:

The Solari are the force of nature that is more akin to humans. They craft and build things, they live in houses they built themselves on top of the Folk Tree City and are slowly developing technology. While this technology is still primitive, it starts to depart from nature. They can fight, but it is not their main power. On a deeper level: they want to develop their ego and mental state. They are focused on the conscious processes of the mind.

The Lunari are the force of nature that resemble the wild side of nature. They live in burrows, tree branches and crowns, and other natural places. They have excellent senses, they are brutal, and while primitive, they are wise, because living aligned with nature, makes you wise. They are formidable warriors. On a deeper level: they want to be in contact with nature and themselves, therefore, with the spirit. They are focused on the unconscious processes of the mind.

As years went by, the Solari began to defy the laws of nature, and nature started bending on itself. The Wild Beasts emerged, wreaking havoc and doing harm to other animals. These are non-intelligent creatures based on the elements - air, earth, water, fire - and are fixated solely on destruction, and started sowing chaos on different parts of nature and the Folk Tree City. These beasts and their smaller representatives appear mostly at night. Luckily, the Lunari were strong enough to fend them off, but this slowly made the Lunari resentful towards the Solari.

The Lunari asked the Solari to stop using technology in the way they were (more on that later), but the Solari refused and urged the Lunari to keep protecting them. And so, little by little, the differences and tension between the Solari and Lunari increased.

After their pleas were ignored for a while, the Lunari did a ritual to contact the God of nature. They wanted to change the ways the Solari were acting and harming nature, so they asked Him for advice.

The God of nature decided to make all the Lunari fall asleep to stop them from protecting the Solari, so that the Solari would feel the consequences of their actions and would think on how to change their ways. All the Lunari except one, who would act as God’s vessel fell asleep. The likely outcomes would be either:

The Solari will fix the problem, recognize their errors, and stop distancing themselves from nature and harming it through their current development of technology.

The Wild Beasts - and thus, nature - would, sooner or later, end up destroying the Folk Tree City and its technology, allowing nature to continue its course, even without the existence of the Solari or Lunari.

After a day of the Lunari falling asleep, and after a night of struggling to fall asleep, the folk that lived in the villages surrounding the Folk Tree City started seeing aggressive animals getting too close to their town, and went to the city asking for help. Once in the city, one of the representatives of the Solari, who are named princes of the Solari, saw the panicked villagers and talked to them. After that, he decided to ask the highest form of authority in the town, the Elder Council, for advice.

Turns out that The Elder Council already knew what was happening. However, The Elder Council has always been of the mind of not interfering with the course of nature themselves, but are always eager to give advice to those eager to bring change to the world. The very same prince who talked with the villagers, Brightwhisker, gets to know about the ritual that transpired two moons ago, and gets to know that the only way to fix it is by asking the God of Nature, located in the Starbind Glade, to ask why He would do this, and ask what would be required from the Solari to stop it, and defy Him, if need be.

And thus, despite his lack of experience in combat, but full of courage, our protagonist decides to leave the Folk Tree City to get to the Starbind Glade and face God. On his way there, he will find and face the Wild Beasts.
